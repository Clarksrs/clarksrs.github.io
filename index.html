Music

Digital Heaven

Having all the tools to create the music I want is divine bliss. Many people seek happiness in nature, but I have found peace in a blend of the natural and digital realms.

  1. Exiting the Atmospehere
    • This track is design to help ease you in to the more intense experiences to come
  2. Implied Lunacy
    • A fun song for dancing. Is it crazy to spend our time on such things? This song is heavily influenced by Virtual Insanity from Jamiroquai. It's a crazy world we're living in.
  3. Run Away And Join The Circus
    • This song really came about because I wanted to make a song with tuba. It's such a great sound. It replaces bass amazingly. Then there's drums lead and chords. Pretty standard really.
  4. Low Gravity
    • I've been playing lots of sci-fi First Person Shooters. There's always hundreds or thousands of other soldiers but only you can stop the evil invading aliendemons.
  5. Extended Conflict
    • I want to make a heavy song with pretty parts, but this song also repsents cycles of not just war, but all conflict. Is it human nature?
  6. Continue?
    • When you play a video game, you can't even fail permanently. The only choice you have is whether you want to continue or not.
  7. First-Class Villain
    • This track is about an evil person who still does good things sometimes and is respected. Probably the best song I've made.
  8. Showdown Between Swordsmen
    • It's a duel so it's gotta kinda sound like awestern, right?
  9. Racing Through The Junkyard
    • This might be the highest bpm track I've ever made. It's a rip-off of a song from Chrono Trigger.
  10. Recreation (Occasional Acid Flashback)
    • Big Lebowski is my favorite Non-Star Wars movie. I made it before the pandemic and didn't know coughing sounds would end up triggering people so much. Aside form the drums it's all just samples.
